My Internship in Refinery

During my internship at a petroleum refinery, I got the opportunity to understand real industrial operations beyond textbooks. I observed distillation columns, heat exchangers, pumps, and control systems in action.

One of the most exciting parts was visiting the control room where engineers monitor pressure, temperature, and flow rates in real-time. It helped me connect theoretical knowledge of process control with practical applications.

I also learned about safety protocols, emergency shutdown systems (ESD), and plant maintenance procedures. This experience significantly improved my understanding of process engineering.

Internships are extremely important for chemical engineering students as they provide real industry exposure and improve problem-solving skills.
